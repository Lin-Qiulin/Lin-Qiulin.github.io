About Me

Qiulin Lin is a Research Assistant Professor in the Department of Data Science at City University of Hong Kong. His research focuses on pioneering online optimization techniques and developing novel algorithms with significant applications in AI/ML systems, cyber-physical systems, smart power grids, and intelligent transportation. He earned his Ph.D. in Information Engineering from The Chinese University of Hong Kong (CUHK) in 2021, under the supervision of Prof. Minghua Chen . Prior to his doctoral studies, he received his B.Eng. degree in Electronic Engineering and Information Science from the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC) in 2016.

Research Interests Overview

My primary research interests lie at the intersection of online optimization, algorithm design, and their practical applications. I am particularly focused on developing solutions for AI/ML systems, enhancing the efficiency of cyber-physical systems, optimizing smart power grid operations (including energy storage system management and electric vehicle charging), and advancing intelligent transportation systems (such as ride-sharing optimization and heavy-duty truck routing).
